Privacy Policy

Wannabe Cup (WBC) golf tournament app · Last updated July 10, 2026

This policy explains what information the Wannabe Cup app ("WBC," "we," "us") collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. WBC is a private application for participants of the Wannabe Cup, an annual invitational golf tournament. It is operated by the tournament directors, not by a company.

Information we collect

  • Account information. If you sign in with Google or Apple, we receive your name and email address from that provider to create and identify your player account. If you use Sign in with Apple and choose to hide your email, we only ever receive Apple's private relay address.
  • Tournament data. Your player profile (name and handicap index) and the golf scores and statistics you or the tournament directors record for you during play.
  • Notification tokens. If you enable push notifications, we store a device token so we can send you tournament alerts (for example, when a scorecard is signed or a round is finalized).

We do not collect precise location, contacts, photos, payment information, or advertising identifiers. WBC contains no advertising and no third-party analytics or tracking beyond the infrastructure providers described below.

How we use your information

  • To authenticate you and link you to your player profile and tournament history.
  • To display live leaderboards, scorecards, skins, closest-to-the-pin, and pairings.
  • To send push notifications you have opted into.

We do not sell your information, and we do not share it for advertising or marketing.

Service providers

WBC runs on Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore database, and Cloud Messaging) and is hosted on Vercel. These providers process data on our behalf so the app can function. Their handling of that data is governed by their own privacy terms. We do not share your data with any other third parties.

Your scores and the "de-identified record"

The Wannabe Cup keeps historical scoring records across many years. When you delete your account, the personal information that identifies you — your login and your email address — is removed. Your past scores remain as part of the tournament's historical records, attached to an internal player ID that is no longer linked to your email or login. In other words: the numbers stay in the record book; the personal identifiers do not.

Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time, in two ways:

  • In the app: open the account menu (your name, top right) and choose Delete account.
  • On the web: visit wannabecup.com/account-deletion and follow the instructions.

Deleting your account removes your login credentials, the mapping between your login and your profile, your stored email address, and your push-notification tokens. Historical scores are retained as de-identified tournament records as described above.

Data security

Data is transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS) connections and stored in Google Firebase with access restricted to the tournament directors and the authenticated player it belongs to.

Children's privacy

WBC is intended for adult participants of the Wannabe Cup golf tournament. It is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

Contact

Questions about this policy or your data can be sent to privacy@wannabecup.com.
